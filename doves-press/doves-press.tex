	\fontsize{28bp}{35bp}\selectfont {\color{BrickRed}AS KINGFISHERS CATCH FIRE} 
	
	\large{\textit{Gerard Manley Hopkins}}
	
	\vspace{12pt}
	
	As kingfishers catch fire, dragonflies draw flame;
	
	As tumbled over rim in roundy wells	
	
	Stones ring; like each tucked string tells, each hung bell's	
	
	Bow swung finds tongue to fling out broad its name;	
	
	Each mortal thing does one thing and the same:
	
	Deals out that being indoors each one dwells;	
	
	Selves — goes itself; myself it speaks and spells,
	
	Crying Whát I dó is me: for that I came.
	
	\vspace{12pt}	

	I say móre: the just man justices;	
	
	Keeps grace: thát keeps all his goings graces;	
	
	Acts in God's eye what in God's eye he is —	
	
	Chríst — for Christ plays in ten thousand places,
	
	Lovely in limbs, and lovely in eyes not his
	
	To the Father through the features of men's faces.

	% Poem 2
	\newpage

	% Here is another way of lining up the title
	\fontsize{40bp}{40bp}\selectfont {\color{BrickRed}PIED\\BEAUTY} \large{\textit{Gerard Manley Hopkins}}
	
	\vspace{12pt}
	
	Glory be to God for dappled things –

    \quad For skies of couple-colour as a brinded cow;

    \quad\quad For rose-moles all in stipple upon trout that swim;

	Fresh-firecoal chestnut-falls; finches’ wings;

    \quad Landscape plotted and pieced – fold, fallow, and plough;

    \quad\quad And áll trádes, their gear and tackle and trim.

	\vspace{12pt}

	All things counter, original, spare, strange;

    \quad Whatever is fickle, freckled (who knows how?)

    \quad\quad With swift, slow; sweet, sour; adazzle, dim;

	He fathers-forth whose beauty is past change:

    \quad\quad\quad\quad Praise Him.
